Cowboy Cookies

Kate Hopkins, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Description

I'm not sure why these are called Cowboy Cookies... perhaps it is because of their homespun, sturdy, trail-mix qualities. I love chocolate chip cookies, and in my opinion these are the best of the chocolate chip cookie genera! This recipe includes changes I made to make these cookies as healthy as they are delicious.

Yields about 84 cookies

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup refined coconut butter
  • 1/2 cup organic raw sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups unbleached white pastry flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 12 oz semi-sweet chocolate chips (about 1 1/2 cups)
  • 1 cup of chopped nuts (optional)
  • 1 cup of raisins (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350° F
  2. Make sure the coconut butter is warm enough to be soft like butter, but not so warm that it melts
  3. Using an electric mixer, thoroughly cream the coconut butter, sugar, and brown sugar in a large bowl
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, and baking powder
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the butter and sugar mixture and blend well
  6. Mix in the rolled oats
  7. Using a large spoon or your hands, fold in the chocolate chips, nuts, and raisins
  8. Drop generous rounded teaspoons of dough on an unoiled baking sheet, spaced about 4 inches apart
  9. Bake for about 12 minutes, until golden
  10. Place the finished cookies on a cooling rack and allow them to cool completely before storing
